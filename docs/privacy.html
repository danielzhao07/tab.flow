Privacy Policy

Effective March 2026 Version 1.0.0

What We Collect

TabFlow collects the minimum data needed to provide its features. Here is exactly what is accessed:

  • Tab titles, URLs, and favicons — used only to display your tabs in the HUD overlay. Never sent to any server.
  • Tab group names and colors — read locally to show group labels. Never stored remotely.
  • Recently closed tab metadata — read from Chrome's session history to power the "Recently closed" section. Never sent to a server.
  • Your email address — collected only if you choose to create an account, via AWS Cognito.
  • Bookmarks, notes, and workspace data — synced to the cloud only when you are signed in and use those features.
  • Extension settings — stored locally in chrome.storage.local. Optionally synced to the cloud if you are signed in.

What Stays on Your Device

The following data never leaves your browser under any circumstance:

  • Your open tab list — titles, URLs, group info, and activity order (MRU) are kept in extension memory only.
  • Tab thumbnails — captured locally and stored only in memory during the current session.
  • Browsing activity used for frecency sorting — stored only in chrome.storage.local on-device.
  • Snooze schedules — stored locally using chrome.alarms and chrome.storage.local.
  • Search history within the HUD — never recorded at all.

Optional Cloud Sync

Cloud features are entirely optional and require you to sign in. If you never create an account, nothing leaves your device.

When signed in, the following may be synced to our backend database:

  • Saved workspaces (tab sets you name and save)
  • Bookmarks saved through TabFlow
  • Notes attached to tabs
  • Your extension settings (theme, grid columns, etc.)

You can delete your account and all associated cloud data at any time by contacting us.

Third-Party Services

TabFlow uses the following third-party services. Each is only active when you use the relevant feature.

Groq
Runs the AI assistant. Your query and open tab titles are sent only when you use the AI chat feature.
AWS Cognito
Handles account sign-in. Your email and a hashed password are stored by AWS under their privacy policy.
AWS S3
Used if tab thumbnail syncing is enabled. Images are stored in a private bucket accessible only to your account.
Neon (Postgres)
Cloud database for workspaces, bookmarks, notes, and settings when you are signed in.

Contact & Data Requests

Have questions about your data, want to request deletion, or found a privacy concern? Reach out — we respond within 48 hours.
